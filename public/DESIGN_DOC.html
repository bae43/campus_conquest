Campus Conquest is a turn based strategy game that aims to invoke a sense of solidarity among students as they team up against other colleges.

The game is unique because it's the first to focus on the concept of a large scale team making decisions as opposed to individuals. A game takes place over a several day period with turns occuring every six hours. All moves that are input within that time are taken in to compute the outcome of that turn. It's a game that everyone can get involved in. It's approachable, casual, and provides a relatable experience to players for being campus-centric.

Goals:

  • Effectively have a game were many players can work together - no one can win by themselves and each person contributes
  • Have a strong sense of setting on Cornell

Actions

Actions are the verbs that we talked about in class. Identify the most important verbs in your game. These verbs should be those that either bring the player closer to achieving the objective, or overcome the challenge. Remember our discussion about the differences between core verbs and verb proxies. A core verb should have an outcome that directly makes progress; it is not a verb that causes another action to happen. Do not use this section to specify the mechanics of your verbs; that will come later. Simply list the verbs and give a one sentence description of each. For each verb, you should identify how important it is, namely critical, valuable, or desirable. A critical verb has to be implemented, else there is no game. Valuable verbs are likely to be implemented, and their loss will probably require some major changes in gameplay. Desirable verbs are the ones that you are most likely to drop if you run out of time. Be honest. If all your verbs are critical, then say so. Do not list a critical verb as desirable just because you want a verb of each type. You may want to use a table to present this information. The primary challenge is to take control of territories. Given only one team can control a territory at a time, there is a constant struggle for power as each time one team advances towards their objective, another is set back.

Players primarily initiate attacks to advance their cause. There is always some risk that their move will be be a failure and result in a greater loss than not making the move at all. The challenge involves skill by judging risks.

Mechanics

Attack

To attack, a player selects a start point (one of their own) and an endpoint (one of their opponents). A limitation is that the two territories must be adjacent. At the end of the turn, the outcome for the action is that the units either capture the opponents territory or they are lost.

Purchases

At the start of every turn, players may either purchase units or powerups. Two limited commodities/currencies would be used. Limitations are being blocked from purchasing without enough resources. Immediate outcomes are having items to place on the board and the newly aquired pieces further advancing the players ability to attack with lower risk of loss.

Interactions

Attack

Trigger is an Attack being issued. The outcome is either the attacker or defender is deemed a winner. Players can increase strength of their army to help control outcome.

Army is buffed

A player adding more units or a powerup will trigger an immediate increase in the army strength. Players can choose to or not to do this and which sections of the board they want to focus on.
